Stamps  Of  The  World

Showcasing a wide range of stamps from all corners of the world and periods of modern history

Country: Dominion of Canada

Issued: 1947

Denomination: 4 Canadian cents

The stamp features an image of Graham Bell, as well as a winged figure crowning his likeness. The telephone lines in the background suggest his invention. Dr. Alexander Graham Bell (1847-1922) emigrated to Canada in 1870 with his family to Paris, Ontario and later at "Tutela Heights," a community near Brantford, Ontario. During a visit there in 1874 he invented the telephone. The first telephone was manufactured in 1875 in Boston, but Dr. Bell gave his first successful demonstration of transmitting speech (one way) over a telegraph wire between Brantford and Paris, Ontario. Interesting enough, Bell favoured the greeting “hoy hoy” for use over the phone instead of “hello”
