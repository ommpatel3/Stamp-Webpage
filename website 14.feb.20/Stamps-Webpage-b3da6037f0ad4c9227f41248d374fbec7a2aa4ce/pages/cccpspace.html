Country: Union of Soviet Socialist Republics

Issued: 1964

Denomination: 50 Soviet kopeks

This stamp commemmorates the the first multimanned spacecraft, Voskhod 2, which carried out its mission in 1964. The stamp features the 3 crew members of the spacecraft: (left to right) Engineer Captain Col. Vladimir Komarov, Technical Sciences Cosmonaut Konstantin Feoktistov and Doctor Cosmonaut Boris Yegorov. The Voshkod program succeeded the Vostok program and used many of the leftover components after that program's cancellation. It was designed my Sergei Korolev, the same man who led the development of Sputnik, the first satellite. Interestingly enough, popular legend states that Kruschev put forward the idea of sending a multi-manned crew into space after hearing about the 2 and 3 manned Apollo and Gemini spacecraft. Due to this rush, many "corners" were cut in the program.
