Stamps  Of  The  World

Showcasing a wide range of stamps from all corners of the world and periods of modern history

Country: Republic of Colombia

Issued: 1944-1945

Denomination: 5 Colombian centavos

This stamp has an image of the coffee harvest in the background. What sets this stamp apart from many others like it is the overprint on this stamp, of Stalin, Roosevelt and Churchill, which was done to commemorate an allied victory of WWII. Even today, the coffee industry produces about 2 billion dollars yearly, accounting for about 8% of Colombia’s GDP. In the past, this number was even higher as certain other industries like petroleum was not as developed. In terms of the overprint, it was generally done in one of three colours, green, red or blue, this one being the last. Also, interestingly enough the bottom of the stamp reads “American Bank Note Co.” which is the company which printed this issue of stamps.
