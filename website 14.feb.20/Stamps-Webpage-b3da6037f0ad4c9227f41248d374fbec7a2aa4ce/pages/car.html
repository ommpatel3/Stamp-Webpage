Country: United States of America

Issued: 1988

Denomination: 25 US cents

The Packard Light Eight was intended to be a high-middle class luxury car. Sold only in 1932, the car was meant to compete with models built by companies such as Buick and Chrysler and compete for their consumer base. Due to the release of the car being right in the middle of the Great Depression, it was a massive failure. The model was considered a failure as it could not lure away buyers from its rivals. Infact, it hurt sales of Packard's volume line, called the Standard Eight.
